Visualizations

The dataset includes all closed (successful or unsuccessful) solicitations with an add date on or after 7/1/2011. Data is obtained from the first tab of the Booth solicitation history report (saved as a .csv).

#### Run Rscript1 ----
source("Rscript1 - data load and transform.R")

Begin with some visualizations of time in each stage:

Theres extremely obvious bimodality in most cases. Note in particular that while PG is skewed positive overall, there seem to be two groups: takes a while and takes a long while. The purple Days from ask to actual is about as perfect a mixture of two \(N(\mu_{k},\sigma^{2}_{k})\) as Ive ever seen. Might be interesting to see if a certain population just takes longer.

There isnt much data in the oral pledge stage so things are a bit bumpier here.

Booked indicates whether the solicitation came in at \(>\)$0, and FY.plan, FY.clear, FY.ask, FY.oral indicate whether the solicitation closed in the same fiscal year as that stage was reached. Combinations of these five indicators will be the dependent variables. plan2actual, clear2actual, ask2actual, oral2actual are measured in days. The other covariates should be self-explanatory.

Variable exploration

Dependent variables

with(mdat, boxplot(plan2actual, clear2actual, ask2actual, oral2actual))

Decidedly right-skewed. Try a square root transformation:

with(mdat, boxplot(sqrt(plan2actual), sqrt(clear2actual), sqrt(ask2actual), sqrt(oral2actual)))
